# Auckland Islands

AUCKLAND ISLANDS, a group in the Pacific Ocean, discovered in 1806 by
Captain Briscoe, of the English whaler "Ocean," in 50 deg. 24' S., 166
deg. 7' E. The islands, of volcanic origin, are very fertile, and are
covered with forest. They were granted to the Messrs Enderby by the
British government as a whaling station, but the establishment was
abandoned in 1852. The islands belong politically to New Zealand.

# Birjend

BIRJEND, the capital of Káïn, a sub-province of Khorasan in Persia, in
32° 53' N. 59° 10' E., and at an elevation of 4550 ft. Pop. about
25,000. It is situated 328 m. from Meshed by the direct road, in a
fertile valley running east and west, of which the southern boundary is
a lofty range of barren hills known as Kuh i Bakeran. Through the valley
runs the Khusp river, which loses itself in the desert towards the west;
it is, however, generally dry. The water-supply of the town and of the
70 or 80 villages under its jurisdiction is very scanty. On the east of
the town at the foot of a hill stands a dilapidated fort. Birjend has
six good caravanserais, a college and some mosques; post and telegraph
offices were established there in 1902.

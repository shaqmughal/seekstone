# Goldingen

GOLDINGEN (Lettish, _Kuldiga_), a town of Russia, in the government of
Courland, 55 m. by rail N.E. of Libau, and on Windau river, in 56 deg.
58' N. and 22 deg. E. Pop. (1897) 9733. It has woollen mills, needle and
match factories, breweries and distilleries, a college for teachers, and
ruins of a castle of the Teutonic Knights, built in 1248 and used in the
17th century as the residence of the dukes of Courland.
